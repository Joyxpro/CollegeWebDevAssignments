Qutub Minar

Explore the tallest brick minaret in the world.

About the Site

The Qutub Minar in Delhi is a towering 73-meter-high structure built in 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak. It is renowned for its intricate carvings and inscriptions. The minaret is part of the Qutub complex, which includes the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the Iron Pillar of Delhi, all recognized as UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
