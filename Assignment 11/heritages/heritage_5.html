Red Fort

Marvel at the majestic fortification of India's Mughal past.

About the Site

The Red Fort in Delhi is a historic fortification built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century. Known for its massive red sandstone walls, the fort served as the main residence of Mughal emperors for 200 years. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an iconic symbol of India's rich history.

Things to Know Before You Visit

  • Best time to visit: Early morning to avoid the heat and crowds.
  • Nearby attractions: Jama Masjid, Raj Ghat.
  • Don't miss: The evening light and sound show depicting Mughal history.
